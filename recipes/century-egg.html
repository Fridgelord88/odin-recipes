Century Egg

Description

The century egg is an egged that is pickled and encased in clay or mud. It also appears on the documentary Smiling Friends.

Ingredients/Materials

  • 100% lye
  • Salt
  • Eggs (duck or quail)
  • scale
  • plastic wrap
  • modeling clay
  • Glass jar with lid

Steps

  1. Create pickling solution by boiling salt and ly in water until completley dissolved. Place eggs in jar with cooled solution.
  2. Store the jar containing the eggs in an area 60-70 degrees and leave for 10 days
  3. After 10 days remove eggs from the solution
  4. Encase the eggs in clay and store in a sealed plastic bag for 2 weeks
  5. After 2 weeks remove eggs from clay and peel off the shell
  6. They are now ready to be served and enjoyed
